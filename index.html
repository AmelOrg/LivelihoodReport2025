Project 01: LHF MultiSectoral

Support to affected/displaced populations in South Through Food Security, Livelihood and Wash

A total of 225 farmers were registered, including 68 women and 157 men. Three agricultural awareness sessions are conducted additional to one more session upon the farmer's request. Following each session, $100 in cash assistance was distributed to each farmer for a period of three months.

In WASH sector ,1500 MHM kits were distributed on a 3 basis one each month in February ,March and April 2025 to 300 women and 200 girls. 20 sessions were conducted in February 2025 for 500 women and girls .

Regarding Livelihood sector, all project activities were achieved: 17 vocational training were delivered (510 Hours) ,265 beneficiaries were graduated , certificates were designed .

Project 03: LIFE France

Digital Youth Empowerment-Hay El Sellom Center

48 participnats completed 7 month cycle of Digital skills training,in addition to Life skilss, soft skills and PSS.

Out of 48 total participants, 40 (83%) secured internships. Of these, 37 interns (92.5%) worked remotely, while 3 (7.5%) completed on-site placements at the Amel Association or local businesses. Intern commitment was exceptionally high: 65% completed over 200 hours, and the remaining 35% finished 150 hours, demonstrating significant dedication to the program.

"Endline Assessment shows a significant development in the youth’s acquisition of digital marketing skills, both in terms of personal growth, interaction with the labor market, and professional technical abilities."

outreach efforts

72 beneficiaries have been registered

Based on the Labour Market Needs Assessment conducted by E-data, focus was placed on high-demand sectors identified by the study: digital skills, modern agriculture, healthcare, and vocational training. Youth aligned with these sectors and requiring foundational or additional skills to enhance employability were targeted. Institutes, universities, cooperatives, and relevant institutions were engaged to support outreach and strengthen youth capacities in line with labour market needs. This approach enabled youth to directly access the labour market through internships, employment opportunities, or self-employment.

"Coordination with the Lebanese University – Faculty of Business Administration (Beirut) enabled the implementation of an advanced digital marketing training addressing labour market gaps. Youth from Hay El Sellom, from first-year to master’s level, were enrolled, and partnerships with private companies were established to provide practical training, internships, and employment opportunities through formal cooperation agreements."

Project 04: DCA

From Crisis to Strength: Advancing Livelihoods and Protection in Lebanon

The project aims to empower youth with the necessary vocational skills and psychosocial support to help them access employment opportunities that meet current labor market needs.

The project will be implemented in three centers: Hay El Sellom and Haret Hreik in Mount Lebanon, and Nabatieh in the South. The selection of the topics was based on an initial labor market needs assessment, which identified key training areas required in each region.

In parallel, the project focuses on technical and vocational empowerment, as well as psychosocial and life skills support, through sessions on soft skills, life skills, and PSS. Moreover, the project will facilitate youth engagement with the job market by offering internship opportunities, participation in job fairs, and training on project proposal writing, particularly emphasizing economic and social feasibility. Importantly, five innovative youth-led business ideas will be selected to receive seed funding for implementation.

Coming Jan 2026: ARDNA

In partnership with Caritas Switherland, Ardna aim strengthen the resilience of crisis-affected communities in southern Lebanon by providing sustainable livelihood opportunities, employability skills, psychosocial support and access to a multi-functional community center that fosters inclusion, self-reliance and social cohesion—especially women children and youth
